A La Juventud Filipina

¡Alza tu tersa frente,
juventud filipina, en este día!
¡Luce resplandeciente
tu rica gallardía,
bella esperanza de la patria mía!

Vuela, genio grandioso,
y les infunde noble pensamiento,
que lance vigoroso,
más rápido que el viento,
su mente virgen al glorioso asiento.

Baja, con la luz grata
de las artes y ciencias, a la arena,
juventud, y desata
la pesada cadena
que tu genio poético encadena.

Ve que en la ardiente zona
do moraron las sombras, el hispano
esplendente corona,
con pía y sabia mano,
ofrece al hijo de este suelo indiano.

Tú, que buscando subes,
en alas de tu rica fantasía,
del Olimpo en las nubes
tiernísima Poesía,
más sabrosa que néctar y ambrosía

Tú, de celeste acento,
melodioso rival de filomena,
que en variado concento
en la noche serena
disipas del mortal la amarga pena;

Tú, que la pena dura
animas al impulso de tu mente,
y la memoria pura
del genio refulgente
eternizas, con genio prepotente;

Y tú, que el vario encanto
de Febo, amado del divino Apeles,
y de Natura el manto,
con mágicos pinceles
trasladar al sencillo lienzo sueles;

¡Corred! que sacra llama
del genio el lauro coronar espera,
esparciendo la Fama
con trompa pregonera
el nombre del mortal por la ancha esfera.

¡Día, día felice,
Filipinas gentil, para tu suelo!
Al Potente bendice,
que con amante anhelo
la ventura te envía y el consuelo.

A La Juventud Filipina

To The Philippine Youth

Raise your smooth brow,
Filipino youth, on this day!

The poem opens with a powerful call to action, addressed to the Filipino youth. The “smooth brow” symbolizes youthful innocence and potential, and urges the Filipino youth to be proud and confident with their heads held high.

Let your rich gallantry
shine bright,
Beautiful hope of my homeland!

Here, Rizal emphasizes the youth’s “rich gallantry” or their bravery, nobility, and willingness to excel, hinting at their potential for greatness. Rizal portrays the youth here as the “beautiful hope” of the Philippines, hoping and encouraging them that they may strive for a better future for the Philippines.

Fly, magnificent genius,
And instill in them noble thoughts

Here, Rizal personifies the “magnificent genius” as a guiding force that inspires the youth with noble inspirations. It is depicted as a powerful influence that will ignite the youth’s aspiration for greatness.

So that with vigorous force,
Faster than the wind,
Their virgin minds ascend to the glorious seat.

Rizal highlights the need for action and dedication for the youth to achieve their goals, and emphasizes an urgency for the youth to pursue these. The use of “virgin minds” suggests that the potential of the youth has not been fully realized yet, and ascending to the “glorious seat” represents the outcome of having a position of honor and achievement.

Descend, with the pleasant light
Of arts and sciences, into the arena,

Here, Rizal urges the youth to embrace “arts and sciences” or education and knowledge as their tools to fight “into the arena” or for their aspirations.

Youth, and unshackle
The heavy chain
That binds your poetic genius.

The “heavy chain” here, could represent the oppression, ignorance, and lack of opportunity that Filipinos faced during the time of Spanish colonization, hindering the creative potential of the youth.

See how in the fervent zone
Where shadows once resided, the splendid
Spanish crown,

This line is still open to interpretation; it could be a sarcastic reference to the “benefits” that the Spanish rule offered, or hinting at their cruelty instead.

With pious and wise hand,
Is offered to the son of this Indian soil.

“Indian soil” was a term that the Spanish used to refer to the Philippines during the colonial period. Here, Rizal might be criticizing the Spaniards’ self-proclaimed benevolence while maintaining control of the land.

You, who ascend in search,
On the wings of your rich imagination,

This section presents the youth as a generation of artists, dreamers, and thinkers. It emphasizes the power of youth to soar and explore new ideas.

From Olympus in the clouds,
Tender Poetry,
Sweeter than nectar and ambrosia

“Olympus”, in Greek mythology is known to be the home of the gods. This imagery symbolizes a descent from a divine place, or an aspiration for excellence. While “nectar” and “ambrosia” being the food of the gods, signifies the beauty and power of artistry.

You, with a celestial accent,
Melodious rival of Philomela,

Here, Rizal likens the youth to the mythical songbird Philomela, bringing solace and comfort with their music.

Who in varied harmony,
the serene night,
Dissipate the bitter sorrow of mortals;

These lines can also say that Rizal suggests that by combining their voices, the youth can bring about good change, alleviating the suffering of Filipinos from the Spanish oppressors.

You, who with firm impulse
life to enduring pain,
And with potent genius,

Rizal sees the Filipino youth as the hope of the nation; the “firm impulse” suggests a strong determination to bring about change. Despite the challenges that they may face from the colonial oppression and societal issues during that time, the youth possesses the intellect and skills to bring about change.

Eternalize the pure memory
Of resplendent genius;

These lines imply that the youth’s actions and achievements could leave a long lasting legacy. “Resplendent” means dazzling or brilliant, indicating a legacy of extraordinary talent and intellect.

And you, who with varied charm,
by Phoebus, the divine Apelles,

These lines compare the artistic youth to the god Phoebus, who was associated with the arts and Apelles, a renowned Greek painter, suggesting the youths mastery in arts.

And with magical brushes,
Are accustomed to transfer
Nature's mantle to the simple canvas;

The use of “magical brushes” emphasizes the artist’s skill and quality of their work. This describes the artist’s ability to capture the beauty and essence of nature and transfer it onto a canvas, showcasing their skills in art.

Hurry! For the sacred flame
Awaits to crown genius with laurel,

This line creates a sense of urgency, while the “sacred flame” can be interpreted as a symbol of inspiration, and recognition.

Spreading Fame
With heraldic trumpet
The name of the mortal throughout the
wide sphere.

These lines suggest that there is a grand and proud announcement of the youth’s strive for greatness. The overall imagery of this stanza is dramatic and inspiring, creating a sense of urgency and aspiration to the Filipino youth.

O happy day,
Gentle Philippines, for your soil!

These lines express joy and optimism, while also addressing the Philippines directly and celebrating its potential.

Bless the Almighty,
Who with loving ardor
Sends you fortune and solace.

Here, there is a direct appeal to a higher power for blessings, describing them as loving and caring. Overall, there is a sense of gratitude and high optimism for the future.
