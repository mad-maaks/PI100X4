Ang Tanglaw ng Bayan

Ang hiningang mapagpala ng matinong edukasyon
Ay may bisang mapang-akit, bisang laging dumadaloy,
Inang-Baya'y binubusog sa biyayang mayamungmong,
Inaangat hanggang ito'y sa pedestal maituntong;

Rizal begins by emphasizing the importance of good education, and how it is a powerful force that continuously influences people. He further explains how the nation can flourish and achieve greatness through it.

At kung paanong ang bulaklak na wari ba'y naluluoy
Ay pamuling sumisigla pag ang hangi'y sumisimoy,

Rizal, then, compares flowers being revived by a gentle breeze to a nation prospering through education.

Iyang tao ay ganyan din: umuunlad, sumusulong,
Edukasyon ang sa kanya'y nagbubunsod sa pagsibol.

Here, Rizal says that the same principle from the last line also applies to people. Education is the reason for their progress and development. The lines here emphasize Rizal’s belief that education is the catalyst for growth and change of the Filipinos.

Tao'y handang tumalikod sa ginhawang pamumuhay,
Kung sa gayon yaong nasang edukasyo'y makakamtan,

These lines show how there are people who are willing to sacrifice comfort, and even endure hardships in order to pursue education.

Edukasyon sa siyensiya at sa sining ay nagluwal
Upang putong na tanghalin ng sa taong karunungan:

He then explains that education brings knowledge in various fields such as in science and arts. This produces knowledgeable individuals crowned with wisdom.

Kung paano ngang sa taluktok ng mataas na tagaytay
Nagmumula yaong batis na malinis at malinaw,
Edukasyon ay ganyan din: lupang kanyang panahanan
Ay lalasap ng biyaya ng payapang kabuhayan.

Here, Rizal uses the image of mountain tops as the source of streams of water in order to symbolize the flow of knowledge and wisdom from education. Like the clear stream, this flow of knowledge enriches the land it touches. This further emphasizes the importance of a good education system for a nation to enjoy a peaceful and prosperous life.

Edukasyon, pag naghari, kabataan ay lulusog,
Ang katawan at ang isip ay uunlad nang maayos,
Kamalia'y masusupil, diwa niya'y mabubusog
Sa marangal na damdaming sa kaluluwa'y humuhubog;

Rizal discusses the transformative power of education on the youth. That when education prevails, they shall then flourish. He further explains how education suppresses ignorance and inspires noble ideas instead.

Tanang bisyo'y mapapawi't sapilitang mauubos;
Umaamo kahit na nga mga bansang asal-hayop;
Ang mabangis, nagbabago at bayaning nababantog.

He then explains that education has the power to civilize the most uncultivated societies, transforming individuals into heroes. This stanza basically focuses on the idea that education refines individuals, which leads to the betterment of them and the society they belong to.

At kung paanong iyang batis, sa pag-agos na marahan
Naglalagos sa makahoy na bukiri't kaparangan,
Ay may dulot na aruga at saganang pagmamahal
Sa halama't mga damong nilalaro't hinahagkan,
Binubusog sa kalinga ang magandang kalikasan;

Rizal continues the metaphor of the gentle stream as education, where it nourishes and revitalizes all parts of society. He emphasizes here the nurturing qualities of education, providing growth to everything it touches.

Ang matinong edukasyon, kapag ito ay nakamtan,
Ang sa tao'y maluluklok sa mataas na pedestal
Na mahigpit pa sa gintong kapuriha't karangalan.

He then explains how achieving proper education places individuals on a high pedestal of honor and respect. This surpasses even the value of gold, and highlights how enduring and far-reaching the benefits of education are.

At ang kanyang mga labi'y dadaluyang walang likat
Ng tubig ng kabaitang kristal wari ang katulad,

Here, the lines show how an educated individual speaks with kindness and clarity, much like crystal-clear waters.

TAt ang kanyang pananalig ay titibay at tatatag,
Ang lakas ng kasama'y masusupil niyang ganap,
Katulad ng mga along di gaanong nagluluwat,
Pagsapit na sa baybayi'y nawawala't nababasag,

Rizal explains that their faith and beliefs are strengthened through education, enabling them to fight and overcome evil. He further uses the imagery of waves dissipating upon reaching the shore to solidify this point. It symbolizes how educated individuals can dispel negative influences.

At ang kanyang halimbawa ay salaming maliwanag
Ng balana sa paghakbang sa pasulong at pataas.

Rizal then concludes his poem by highlighting that an educated person’s life serves as a clear example for others, inspiring them to progress and aspire for greater achievements.
