El Agua y El Fuego

Agua somos, decís; vosotros fuego;
Como lo queráis, ¡sea!...
Vivamos en sosiego,
Y el incendio jamás luchar nos vea;
Sino que unidos por la ciencia sabia
De las calderas en el seno ardiente,
Sin cóleras, sin rabia,
Formemos el vapor, quinto elemento,
¡Progreso, vida, luz y movimiento!

Water and Fire

Water are we, you say, and yourselves fire;

The poem starts by setting up a metaphor. One group is likened to water, while the other to fire. With the nature of the two elements, this could be a representation of two opposing forces. From the context of when this poem was written, we can say that “water” is the Philippines, while “fire” is the Spanish.

so let us be what we are
and co-exist without ire,
and may no conflagration ever find us at war.

Here, Rizal calls for acceptance of differences, that both sides should embrace their nature without trying to change each other. He pleads for a peaceful coexistence, and wishes for the end of violent conflict or war between the two forces.

But rather, fused together by cunning science
within the cauldrons of the ardent breast,

Rizal, here suggests a more constructive approach. Instead of conflict, the two forces should work together.

without rage, without defiance,
do we form steam, fifth element indeed:
progress, life, enlightenment, and speed!

Rizal reiterates here the need for peace and cooperation. He further emphasizes this by using the idea of the creation of a fifth element, beyond the classical four: earth, air, fire, and water, if the two forces were to join together. These passages could reflect Rizal’s belief at the time of writing this, that real change and reform from Spain is the key to the Philippines' betterment.
